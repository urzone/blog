Auto Insurance

XiaO / 2024-02-27

Liability insurance

Bodily Injury (BI) Limit: $50k/$100k

If you are at fault in a covered accident and injure someone, this coverage pays up to a per person and per accident limit.

Property Damage: $10,000

If you are at fault in a covered accident, this pays for damage to property up to a specified limit.

Personal Injury Protection (pip): $250,000

Personal injury protection, also known as “no-fault insurance,” pays the medical bills and rehabilitative costs for you and your passengers after you’re injured in a car accident.

Property Protection: $1,000,000

Property Protection coverage will pay for damage incurred to properly parked vehicles or other tangible property of another, regardless of fault. Damage to vehicles and their contents, including trailers, is excluded except when properly parked. This coverage is subject to the terms, limits, and conditions of the policy contract.

Uninsured Liability

Bodily Injury (BI) Limit: Not Covered

Pays up to a per person and per accident limit for accidents caused by other motorists who don’t carry coverage for the extent of your injuries that occurred.

Underinsured Bodily Injury Limit: Not Covered

Pays up to a per person and per accident limit for accidents caused by other motorists who don’t carry coverage for the extent of your injuries that occurred.

Auto Protection

Comprehensive Deductible: Not Covered

Damage to your vehicle by theft, fire, hail, flood or vandalism. You pay only the deductible.

Collision Deductible: Not Covered

Damage to your vehicle is covered up to its actual cash value if you overturn or collide with another car or object other than an animal. You pay only the deductible.

Rental Reimbursement: Not Covered

Covers car rental expenses while repairs are being completed as a result of a covered loss. There is a per day and maximum limit.

Towing & Labor: Not Covered

Covers the labor to change a flat tire, deliver emergency fuel, or tow the vehicle to the nearest repair facility if it is not operable.

Loan/Lease GAP coverage: Not Covered

Loan/lease coverage is a variant of gap insurance coverage. It’s similar to gap coverage in that it’s designed to cover the gap between what you owe on your car and its actual cash value.

Collision Deductible Waiver: Not Covered
